Biography

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Chemistry at the National Institute of Technology Karnataka (NITK) under the supervision of Dr. Lakshmi Vellanki. My doctoral dissertation, titled "π-Conjugated Porous Polymers with Ionic and Luminescent Cores for the Detection and Removal of Environmental Pollutants," explored novel architectures for environmental remediation.

Prior to my Ph.D., I achieved University 1st Rank in my Master of Science (General Chemistry) from Karnatak University, Dharwad. I possess strong expertise in single and multi-step organic synthesis, anhydrous chemistry, and synthesis and structural elucidation of complex reticular porous material frameworks. Beyond synthesis, I am experienced in theoretical DFT calculations using Gaussian, molecular docking, and advanced analytical characterization techniques.

Core Research Areas

Zwitterionic CPPs & Iodine Capture

Developing novel zwitterionic conjugated porous polymers incorporating croconic and squaric acids. This creates a synergistic donor-acceptor environment, enabling massive iodine/polyiodide vapour adsorption capacities (up to 6.4 g/g) through electrostatic, π–π, and charge-transfer interactions.

Luminescent CPPs as Sensors

Integrating β-diketone boron difluoride (BF2) into olefin-linked luminescent CPPs using Knovenagel condensation. These materials serve as highly sensitive fluorescence sensors for nitroaromatic explosive compounds (like picric acid) via the Perrin’s sphere of action quenching method.

Cationic Adsorbents & Energy

Synthesis of cationic CPPs based on pyrylium and thio-pyrylium networks for the highly efficient, counter-ion mediated removal of organic dyes in aqueous media. Also developing quinoxaline-based ladder-type 1D polymers for supercapacitor applications.
